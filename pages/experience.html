A day in the life of a CS Major

Do a lot of CS stuff

Being a computer scientist mostly includes writing code, and fixing said code. You will either be adding new features or bug fixing, most likely you will do both and switch between them regularly. While bug fixing you will replicate the bug and try to find the source. This is where problem solving comes into play in that the code base will be hundreads of thousands of lines of code if not millions. You have to be able to narrow down the files that the issue may be in.

Other than programming, you will have daily meetings with your team to discus what you have been working on and any problems you may be encountering. This serves to keep the team up to date on the status of the project, keep anyone from getting stuck, and propell the team forward by sharing your knowledge. These meetings are kept short to force them to be efficent and not waste time to improve the project.

You might also assist in writing documentation or communicating with the customer and stakeholders about the status of the project and any new features they want.
